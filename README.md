# Funnel-and-Retention-Analysis---MercadoLibre
context:In this role, I took on the position of data analyst within Walmart's sales team, supporting strategic decisions related to budget allocation, inventory optimization, and store space utilization.
The business question wasn't simply "What sells best?" but rather:
Which departments generate the greatest value relative to the space they occupy?
Where are we underutilizing resources?
How can we prioritize investment to maximize return?

analysis:I integrated sales, store, and department data and built key KPIs:
Efficiency (sales/m²): measures space profitability
Share: each department's weight in the total
Opportunity Index: identifies high volume with low efficiency
I performed comparisons by store type, performance rankings, and holiday impact analysis.

conclusions:Decoupling between volume and efficiency
Not all top-selling departments are the most efficient.

→ I identified categories with high volume but low yield per unit of space.
Revenue concentration
A small group of departments generates the majority of sales (Pareto principle).
Impact of store type
Larger A-type stores are not always the most efficient.

→ Size does not guarantee better relative performance.
Holiday effect
Sales increases were identified in specific weeks, but these were not uniform across departments.
